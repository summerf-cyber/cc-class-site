# Buyer Persona: Marcus Chen

## Overview

| Field | Details |
|---|---|
| **Name** | Marcus Chen *(composite persona)* |
| **Title** | VP of Procurement / Chief Procurement Officer / Director of Strategic Sourcing |
| **Industry** | Aerospace & Defense, Advanced Manufacturing, Enterprise Technology |
| **Company Size** | $500M – $5B annual revenue |
| **Team Size** | 8–40 direct/indirect reports |
| **Age Range** | 42–58 |
| **Education** | BS in Supply Chain, Engineering, or Finance; often an MBA |

---

## Who He Is

Marcus oversees a sourcing and procurement function that touches hundreds of suppliers, millions in spend, and a constant barrage of cost pressures from above. His days are split between supplier negotiations, internal stakeholder management (engineering, finance, operations), and trying to pull actionable insight out of fragmented ERP data, Excel exports, and outdated cost models. He is accountable for hard savings targets — typically 3–8% annual cost reduction — and increasingly for should-cost accuracy, supplier risk exposure, and supply chain resilience.

---

## Core Responsibilities

- Owns total cost of ownership (TCO) analysis and should-cost modeling across commodity categories
- Manages supplier relationships and contract negotiations across complex, multi-tier supply chains
- Drives cross-functional alignment between engineering, manufacturing, and finance on cost targets
- Reports procurement ROI and savings attainment to the C-suite
- Navigates commodity volatility (steel, aluminum, rare earths, semiconductors, energy)
- Supports new product introduction (NPI) with early cost visibility

---

## Primary Pain Points

- **Cost visibility gaps** — should-cost models are built manually in Excel, rely on stale data, and take days or weeks to produce
- **Data fragmentation** across SAP, Oracle, supplier quotes, commodity indexes, and spreadsheets with no single source of truth
- **Slow time to insight** — savings windows are often missed before models are ready
- **Difficulty defending sourcing decisions** to finance without rigorous, audit-ready cost breakdowns
- **Experienced buyers spending 60–70% of their time** on data wrangling instead of strategic work
- **Relentless commodity volatility** with no real-time market intelligence integrated into cost models

---

## Goals & Success Metrics

- Hit or exceed annual cost savings targets (hard dollar and cost avoidance)
- Reduce should-cost modeling time from days to hours
- Improve negotiation outcomes with data-backed positions
- Position procurement as a strategic partner to engineering and finance
- Reduce dependency on consultants for cost benchmarking

---

## Buying Behavior

Skeptical, ROI-driven buyer who has been burned by enterprise software promises before. Not the sole decision-maker — finance, IT, and the CFO are typically involved, requiring **3–6 stakeholders to sign off**.

Evaluation process includes:
- Hands-on pilot or POC
- Peer reference checks
- Demonstration using his own data and commodity categories

Responds to concrete case studies with hard dollar savings figures, time-to-value clarity, and realistic implementation timelines. Skeptical of vague "AI-powered" claims, tools that require heavy lift, and vendors who can't speak fluently about manufacturing cost structures.

| Layer | Tools |
|---|---|
| **ERP** | SAP, Oracle, or Infor |
| **Sourcing** | Coupa, Jaggaer, or Ariba |

Pragmatic technology adopter who prioritizes:
- Clean integration with existing stack
- Buyer-friendly UX that doesn't require data science expertise
- Results he can present in a board deck

---

## Emotional Drivers

- Wants to transform procurement from a cost center into a **competitive advantage**
- Wants to walk into negotiations knowing more about a supplier's cost structure than the supplier expects
- Wants his CFO to trust his numbers and his team to stop drowning in spreadsheets

---

## Key Phrases He Uses

> "I need to know if we're leaving money on the table."

> "My team spends more time building models than using them."

> "I can't go to the CFO with a number I can't defend."

> "We need should-cost capability that doesn't require a consultant every time."

> "How does this integrate with SAP?"

---

## Landing Page Implications

| Principle | Application |
|---|---|
| **Lead with outcomes** | e.g., "Reduce should-cost modeling time by 80%" |
| **Use procurement-native language** | TCO, should-cost, commodity volatility, NPI |
| **Build credibility fast** | Logos and case studies from recognizable manufacturing or aerospace companies |
| **Low-friction CTA** | Offer a proof-of-concept path rather than a generic "Request a Demo" |
| **Address objections early** | ERP integration and implementation timeline concerns are buying objections disguised as technical questions |
